Two Types of Claims

Death Claim: Filed by the nominee/legal heir after the policyholder's death. LIC pays the sum assured + bonuses to the nominee tax-free.

Maturity Claim: Filed by the policyholder when the policy completes its term. LIC pays the maturity benefit + accumulated bonuses. See the Maturity Help page for this process.

This page covers Death Claims. If you are in this situation, please call Vinodbhai directly — he responds personally, day or evening.

Step by Step

LIC Death Claim Process — Step by Step

  • 1
    Inform Vinodbhai immediately

    Call or WhatsApp Vinodbhai as soon as possible after the death. He will guide you on the exact process, required documents, and LIC branch to visit. You do not need to figure this out alone.

  • 2
    Obtain the Death Certificate

    A registered Death Certificate from the Municipal Corporation or Gram Panchayat is mandatory. Vinodbhai will tell you the exact format LIC requires.

  • 3
    Locate the policy bond

    Find the original LIC policy document. If it is lost, Vinodbhai can help you obtain a duplicate — this does not stop the claim process.

  • 4
    Fill LIC Form 3783 — Claim Form A

    The main death claim form. Vinodbhai fills this with you — ensuring no errors that could delay the claim. This form requires details about the policyholder, date/cause of death, and nominee information.

  • 5
    Submit documents to the LIC servicing branch

    All documents are submitted to the LIC branch that services the policy. Vinodbhai accompanies families to the branch and handles the submission personally.

  • 6
    Track claim status and follow up

    LIC typically processes claims within 30 days of complete documentation. Vinodbhai follows up with the branch on your behalf until the settlement is complete.

  • 7
    Receive claim amount in nominee's bank account

    LIC transfers the claim amount directly to the nominee's bank account (NEFT). Vinodbhai helps if there are any account verification steps.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does LIC take to settle a death claim?

LIC is required to settle claims within 30 days of receiving complete documentation. If there is any investigation, it can take up to 90 days. Vinodbhai follows up actively to ensure your claim is not delayed.

What if the nominee is a minor?

If the nominee is under 18, the claim is paid to the appointed guardian. This must be declared in advance (in the policy or separately). Vinodbhai helps families navigate minor nominee situations.

Can a death claim be rejected?

Yes — the most common rejection reasons are: non-disclosure of medical condition at the time of buying the policy, lapsed policy (premium not paid), or suicide within the waiting period (typically 1–2 years). Vinodbhai reviews all cases before submission to minimise rejection risk.

The policy bond is lost. Can we still claim?

Yes. Vinodbhai helps you obtain a duplicate policy bond from LIC before submitting the claim. This adds a few days but does not prevent the claim.

Is the LIC death claim amount taxable?

No. Death claim proceeds received by the nominee are fully tax-free under Section 10(10D) of the Income Tax Act — regardless of the amount.
